Matcha Cheesecake

Description

A rich, creamy baked cheesecake infused with premium matcha green tea powder. It has a smooth texture, a subtle earthy flavor, and just the right amount of sweetness. Finished with a light dusting of matcha, it's an elegant dessert that's perfect for tea lovers.

Matcha cheesecake combines the rich, creamy texture of classic cheesecake with the earthy, slightly bitter flavor of premium Japanese matcha. Made with a buttery biscuit crust and a smooth cream cheese filling, it offers a perfect balance of sweetness and the distinctive notes of green tea. Light, velvety, and elegant, it's a refreshing dessert that pairs beautifully with coffee, tea, or enjoyed on its own.

Ingredients

Crust

  • 200 g digestive biscuits (or graham crackers), crushed
  • 90 g unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 tbsp sugar (optional)

Filling

  • 600 g cream cheese, softened
  • 180 g granulated sugar
  • 200 ml heavy cream
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 2 tbsp high-quality matcha powder
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Pinch of salt

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 160°C (320°F).
  2. Mix the crushed biscuits with the melted butter until evenly combined.
  3. Press the mixture firmly into the bottom of a springform pan and chill for 15–20 minutes.
  4. Beat the cream cheese until smooth.
  5. Add the sugar and mix until fully incorporated.
  6. Sift in the matcha powder and cornstarch, then mix well.
  7. Stir in the vanilla extract, lemon juice, heavy cream, and a pinch of salt until smooth. Avoid overmixing.
  8. Pour the filling over the chilled crust.
  9. Bake for 50–60 minutes, or until the edges are set and the center still has a slight wobble.
  10. Turn off the oven, crack the door open, and let the cheesecake cool inside for about 1 hour.
  11. Refrigerate for at least 6 hours, preferably overnight.
  12. Dust with matcha powder before serving and garnish with whipped cream or fresh berries if desired.
